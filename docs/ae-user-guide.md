## Logging In

1. Open the Branch360 Google Sheets application
2. Your account is automatically authenticated using your Google account email
3. The system will recognize your role as Account Executive
4. You'll be taken directly to your personalized dashboard

**Note:** If you don't see your dashboard, contact your administrator to ensure your account is set up correctly.

## Viewing Your Dashboard

Your AE Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of your daily and monthly performance:

### Today's Metrics
- **TAP Goal vs Actual**: Track your daily Talk-to-Appointment goals
- **Appointments Set**: Number of appointments scheduled today
- **Appointments Completed**: Number of appointments you've completed
- **Quotes Created**: Total quotes generated today
- **Quotes Won**: Successful conversions today
- **Sales Goal vs Actual**: Your daily revenue target and current performance

### Month-to-Date Metrics
- Total TAP for the month
- Total appointments completed
- Total quotes created and won
- Win rate percentage
- Total sales revenue
- Days active this month

### Pipeline Overview
Your pipeline is organized by stage:
- **Proposal**: Opportunities with proposals sent
- **Negotiation**: Active negotiations
- **Sold**: Recently closed deals
- **On Hold**: Paused opportunities

### New Leads
- Leads automatically assigned to you based on zip code territory
- Shows customer name, address, contact info, and submission date
- Click on a lead to view full details and convert to opportunity

### Recent Activity
- Latest updates to your opportunities
- Recent wins and losses
- Status changes

### Upcoming Tasks
- Proposals needing follow-up (older than 7 days)
- Opportunities requiring attention

## Submitting Daily Activity

**When:** Submit your daily activity at the end of each workday

**How:**
1. Navigate to the Daily Activity section on your dashboard
2. Enter the following metrics:
   - **TAP Goal**: Your target for the day (default: 10)
   - **TAP Actual**: Number of talk-to-appointments completed
   - **Appointments Set**: New appointments scheduled
   - **Appointments Completed**: Appointments you attended
   - **Quotes Created**: Number of quotes generated
   - **Quotes Won**: Successful conversions
   - **Quote Value**: Total value of quotes created
   - **Sales Goal**: Your daily revenue target
   - **Sales Actual**: Actual revenue for the day

3. Click "Save Daily Activity"
4. The system will update your metrics and calculate your win rate automatically

**Tips:**
- Be accurate and honest with your numbers
- Submit before end of day to ensure accurate reporting
- Review your month-to-date progress regularly

## Managing Your Pipeline

### Viewing Your Pipeline
Your pipeline shows all active opportunities organized by stage. Each opportunity displays:
- Customer name and address
- Annual value
- Current stage
- Proposal date
- Point of contact information
- Notes

### Updating Opportunity Stages
1. Select an opportunity from your pipeline
2. Update the stage:
   - **Proposal**: Initial proposal sent
   - **Negotiation**: Actively negotiating terms
   - **Sold**: Deal closed successfully
   - **Dead**: Opportunity lost
   - **On Hold**: Temporarily paused

3. Add notes about the update
4. Save changes

### Searching Opportunities
Use the search function to find specific opportunities by:
- Customer name
- Address
- Point of contact name
- Entry ID

You can also filter by:
- Stage
- Date range

## Converting Leads

When a technician submits a lead in your territory, it's automatically assigned to you.

### Viewing New Leads
1. Check the "New Leads" section on your dashboard
2. Leads show:
   - Customer name and service address
   - Zip code
   - Contact information (phone, email)
   - Service type
   - Notes from the technician
   - Submission date

### Converting a Lead to Opportunity
1. Click on a lead to view full details
2. Review the information provided by the technician
3. Click "Convert to Opportunity"
4. The system will:
   - Create a new opportunity in your pipeline
   - Set the stage to "Proposal"
   - Mark the lead as "Converted"
   - Preserve all customer information

### Marking Leads as Contacted
1. After contacting a lead, click "Mark as Contacted"
2. The lead status updates to "Contacted"
3. Your last contact date is recorded

**Best Practice:** Convert leads to opportunities within 24 hours of assignment.

## Creating Opportunities

You can manually create opportunities for prospects not submitted as leads:

1. Navigate to "Create Opportunity" in your dashboard
2. Fill in the required information:
   - **Customer Name**: Full business name
   - **Service Address**: Complete service location
   - **Zip Code**: 5-digit zip code
   - **Point of Contact**: Name, phone, and email
   - **Source**: How you found this opportunity
   - **Sale Type**: New Business, Renewal, etc.
   - **Service Description**: Type of service needed
   - **Initial Fee**: One-time setup fee
   - **Monthly Fee**: Recurring monthly charge
   - **Frequency**: Service visits per year
   - **Annual Value**: Calculated automatically
   - **Notes**: Any relevant information

3. Click "Create Opportunity"
4. The opportunity appears in your pipeline at the "Proposal" stage

## Best Practices

### Daily Routine
1. **Morning**: Review your dashboard, check new leads, plan your day
2. **During Day**: Log activities as they happen
3. **End of Day**: Submit daily activity metrics

### Lead Management
- Respond to new leads within 4 hours
- Convert qualified leads to opportunities immediately
- Follow up on proposals after 7 days
- Update opportunity stages promptly

### Pipeline Hygiene
- Keep opportunities current and accurate
- Add notes after each customer interaction
- Move dead opportunities to "Dead" stage
- Review and clean up "On Hold" opportunities weekly

### Communication
- Use notes field to document all customer interactions
- Include dates and outcomes in your notes
- Update contact information when it changes

### Performance Tracking
- Monitor your win rate weekly
- Compare actual vs. goal metrics daily
- Review month-to-date progress regularly
- Identify areas for improvement
